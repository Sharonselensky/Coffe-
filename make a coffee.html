Introduction to Coffee Brewing

Great coffee isn’t about expensive machines — it’s about understanding the fundamentals. The perfect cup comes down to four key elements:

Quality Beans

Fresh, whole beans ground just before brewing make the biggest difference.

Water Temperature

195-205°F is ideal. Too hot burns the coffee, too cool under-extracts.

Brew Ratio

Start with 1:16 — 1g coffee to 16g water. Adjust to taste.

Brew Time

Each method has an optimal time. Too fast = weak, too slow = bitter.

Pro Tip

Buy coffee roasted within the last 2-4 weeks for maximum flavor.

Essential Equipment

You don’t need everything at once. Start with the basics and upgrade as you explore.

Coffee Grinder

Burr grinders give consistent grind size. Blade grinders work for beginners on a budget.

Priority: High

Kitchen Scale

Measuring by weight beats volume. A $15 scale improves consistency dramatically.

Priority: High

Kettle

Gooseneck kettles give pour control. Electric with temp control is ideal for pour-over.

Priority: Medium

Brewer

French press, V60, Aeropress, or espresso machine. Choose based on your taste preference.

Priority: High

Thermometer

If your kettle doesn’t show temp, a simple probe thermometer ensures 195-205°F water.

Priority: Low

Timer

Your phone works fine. Precise brew time prevents over or under-extraction.

Priority: Low
Brew Guides

Popular Brewing Methods

Each method brings out different flavors. Pick one to start, then experiment.

French Press

Full-bodied, rich coffee with natural oils. Most forgiving method for beginners.

Ingredients (1 serving)

  • • 30g coarse ground coffee
  • • 500ml water at 200°F
1

Preheat & Add Coffee

Rinse press with hot water. Add coarse grounds to empty press.

2

Bloom

Pour 60ml water, stir gently, wait 30 seconds.

3

Fill & Steep

Add rest of water. Place lid on, don't plunge. Wait 4 minutes.

4

Plunge & Serve

Press plunger down slowly. Serve immediately to avoid over-extraction.

Pour Over

Clean, bright cup that highlights complex flavors. V60 or Chemex methods.

Ingredients (1 serving)

  • • 20g medium-fine ground coffee
  • • 320ml water at 205°F
1

Rinse Filter

Place filter in dripper. Rinse with hot water to remove paper taste.

2

Add Coffee & Bloom

Add grounds. Pour 40ml water in circles. Wait 45 seconds.

3

Main Pour

Pour remaining water slowly in spirals up to 320ml total.

4

Drain & Enjoy

Total brew time: 2:30-3:30. Swirl and serve.

Espresso

Concentrated, intense shot with crema. Base for lattes, cappuccinos, and Americanos.

Ingredients (Double Shot)

  • • 18g fine ground coffee
  • • 36g espresso yield
1

Grind & Dose

Grind fine like table salt. Add 18g to portafilter.

2

Distribute & Tamp

Level grounds. Tamp with 30lbs pressure, keep flat.

3

Extract

Lock in portafilter. Start shot. Target 25-30 seconds for 36g out.

4

Dial In

Too fast = coarser grind. Too slow = finer grind. Aim for balance.

Instant Coffee

Quick and convenient. With the right technique, instant can actually taste good.

Ingredients (1 serving)

  • • 2g instant coffee (1 heaped tsp)
  • • 200ml water
1

Don't Use Boiling Water

Let water cool to 185°F. Boiling water burns instant and makes it bitter.

2

Create a Paste

Mix coffee with 2 tbsp cold water first. Dissolves better, less bitter.

3

Add Hot Water

Pour remaining hot water slowly while stirring.

4

Upgrade It

Add a pinch of salt, cocoa, or cinnamon. Use milk instead of water.

Tips for Better Flavor

Small changes make big differences in your cup.

Use Filtered Water

Coffee is 98% water. Tap water with chlorine or minerals ruins good beans. Use filtered or bottled.

Store Beans Properly

Keep in airtight container away from light, heat, and moisture. Don't freeze or refrigerate.

Preheat Everything

Rinse your brewer, mug, and filters with hot water first. Maintains stable brew temperature.

Adjust One Variable

If coffee tastes off, change only grind size, ratio, or time. Don't change everything at once.

Check Roast Date

Coffee peaks 7-14 days after roasting. Avoid beans older than 30 days for best flavor.

Keep Equipment Clean

Old coffee oils go rancid. Clean grinder monthly and rinse brewers after each use.

Avoid These

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Learn from these before they ruin your brew.

Water above 205°F extracts bitter compounds and burns coffee. Let boiling water sit for 30-45 seconds before pouring, or use a thermometer. Ideal range is 195-205°F.

Too fine for French press = bitter sludge. Too coarse for espresso = sour, weak shot. Match grind to method: coarse for French press, medium for pour-over, fine for espresso.

Coffee absorbs odors and moisture from the fridge. Condensation forms when you take it out. Store in airtight container in cool, dark cupboard instead.

"A scoop" varies wildly. Inconsistent ratios = inconsistent coffee. A $15 scale ensures you use 1:16 ratio every time. Measure both coffee and water by weight.

Supermarket beans often sit for months. Coffee loses 70% of flavor compounds after 30 days. Buy from local roasters and check roast dates. Fresher = better.

Coffee oils build up and turn rancid, adding bitter off-flavors. Rinse after each use, deep clean weekly. Clean grinder monthly to remove built-up fines.
